**Q: What is photosynthesis?**

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants, algae, and some 
bacteria convert light energy from the sun into chemical energy 
stored in glucose. Plants take up carbon dioxide and using 
chlorophyll convert it into energy during the day. Sunlight is 
essential for this process.

Source: jesc105.pdf, Pages 4, 5, 16

**Q: Explain the water cycle.**

The water cycle is a continuous process involving movement of 
water on, above, and below Earth's surface.

1. Evaporation — water changes to vapor using sun's heat
2. Transpiration — plants release water vapor through stomata
3. Condensation — vapor cools and forms clouds
4. Precipitation — water falls as rain, snow, or hail
5. Runoff — water flows back to rivers, lakes, and oceans

Source: jesc105.pdf, Pages 5, 14, 17

**Q: What are chemical reactions?**

Chemical reactions are processes where chemical changes occur, 
leading to transformation of substances. They involve breaking 
and making of bonds between atoms, forming new substances. 
Types include combination, decomposition, exothermic, 
endothermic, and displacement reactions.

Source: jesc101.pdf, Pages 1, 2, 6, 14

**Q: What is the difference between acids and bases?**

Acids release hydrogen ions (H+) in water and have pH less 
than 7. Bases produce hydroxide ions (OH-) and have pH greater 
than 7. The pH scale ranges from 0 to 14 — pH 7 is neutral, 
below 7 is acidic, above 7 is basic.

Source: jesc102.pdf, Pages 10, 13, 17, 18

**Q: How does the human digestive system work?**

Food enters the mouth, moves to stomach through oesophagus. 
Stomach mixes food with digestive juices including hydrochloric 
acid to break down proteins. Small intestine breaks down 
carbohydrates and fats. Villi in small intestine absorb 
digested food into blood. Large intestine absorbs water and 
remaining waste is eliminated.

Source: jesc105.pdf, Pages 6, 7, 8
